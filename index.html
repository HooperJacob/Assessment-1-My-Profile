The Project


Overview

An all-purpose, evolving database software for the Aged Care system, focused more on speed of access and ease of use. Ideally a sponsored, central system to be used and distributed by the government, allowing for consistency and ease of access for doctors across homes, as well as allowing for proper regulation of subsidy funding.

The current issue with a lot of database software used in the Aged Care sector, is that it is not user friendly for the average user in the industry.

Quite a large amount of the Carers and Nurses have not got much technical knowhow, being from an industry that was largely paper focused for their medical records. In some facilities, the change to digital documentation has not been pushed hard until the last 5 years, and even then; some facilities still use paper records in some scenarios when programs fail.

Motivation

This leads into my motivation for creating this program, to solve some of the problems that the personnel in the Aged Care industry face. As I am actually an agency AIN (Assist Nurse), so I actually have experience in the sector, and it is one of the main reasons for my drive to improve the industry through better technological improvement.

My major motivation for creating the software, is making it more time efficient, as well as being more user friendly. More and more people are entering the sector at older ages, with much higher needs for their cares. This is where more efficient database software comes in. With more and more high needs care patients coming in, the amount of time needed to perform their cares will marginally increase in time.

However, quite a few of these programs have not shown increases in efficiency, or integration of modern smart technologies that other sectors are finding positive benefits of.

Project Description

  • Voice to text integration for taking notes, and inputting data. Even some of the best nurses and carers, with excellent time management skills struggle if they have a time-consuming shift due to unforeseen complications. Being able to have voice to text technology in order to save time would be a major benefit for the system as a whole. Rather than inputting the medical data for each resident at the end of the shift/or when you have time, by streamlining some of the process to be voice to text, it could drastically reduce the amount of time spent on paperwork and allow for more time spent with residents.

  • Voice command integration. Similar to popular virtual personal assistants, the program will have basic voice commands in order to facilitate faster operations, with more convenience. Such as pulling medical records to your screen with a voice command or sending messages to doctors about changes in a resident’s health, a lot faster.

  • Simpler, streamlined user interfacing to complement voice to text software, enabling workers to save time inputting information in order to provide higher quality care. An issue some facilities have is lack of data input for medical records, due to lack of experience with the technology needed to input it, or flat-out refusal because carers don’t like the systems in place. By taking the time to design an interface for aged care workers that aren’t so confident with technology, facilities can up the amount of funding they receive by submitting better, more consistent documentation.

  • Proper smart technology integration would be another important feature of the software. While not all facilities may not have the money required to outfit their workplaces with the most modern technology, it’s important to have the integration of smart hardware going forward into the future. As time progresses, more and more innovations are going to be made with technologies, such as smarter GPS devices for those residents who wander their facilities, as well as current innovations, such as the Gaspard pad for wheelchairs. Integration with technology like this could allow for automated pressure documentation, which is currently something that has to be monitored and documented manually into databases.

  • Proper tutorials and education resources. So many services don’t have ready access to tutorials teaching their users how to use their software, outside of proper training. By creating tutorials that are designed for those with lack of experience in mind, it would allow for less time spent with staff confused or having to ask IT personnel how to fix common problems.

  • App integration is something else that would be a strong possibility as a feature for the program. One of the major downfalls of a lot of the hardware and software, is that the developers are greedy, and lack foresight for technological integration of their respective technologies. By allowing app functionality, ease of download for hardware that is commonly found in aged care facilities is achieved, so that no special, gimmicky hardware that is too niche for anything else has to be purchased. Another benefit, is that workers could download the software and have a login feature, even further reducing the hardware costs, and increasing accessibility for the people who use the software.

Resources needed - Tools and Technologies

The software needed to code the program would require the language to have the ability to program voice recognition software, as well as create complex database software, storing sometimes over one hundred resident’s medical records, as well as other relevant information. Ideally, if the software could be created as an app for smart devices, such as Apple or Android software, this would allow the program to bypass the need for unique hardware. The software could also potentially benefit from the use of AI to regulate and find better ways to process the data involved, speeding up the software through establishing trends and other important information. Another important technology needed for such sensitive information is data encryption, as it is extremely sensitive info being supplied into the data base.

Skills Required

The skills required for the programming of this app would be proficient database knowledge, as well as understanding virtual assistant programs that use voice technologies. Another important skill would be the need to communicate with and understand the target audiences needs for the program, as it is designed with people have less experience with technology as a whole. The writer of the software would also need extensive cross platform knowledge, being a program targeted at multiple platforms and hardware types.

Outcome

If this software project was successful, it would help improve the time management, and the ergonomics of the Aged Care industry immensely. By cutting down on the time needed for data input, there could be so much more quality time given to those who need it. As the amount of high care residents increase in the Aged Care industry, reducing the time spent on paperwork would alleviate the stress placed on those working in it. Another effect of the success of this technology will be that having a higher input of documentation through ease of access, the amount of funding for each facility will increase, allowing for more resources to be allocated for a higher level of care.
